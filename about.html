Our Story

Coca-Cola was invented in 1886 by pharmacist John Stith Pemberton in Atlanta, Georgia. It became the world’s most popular soft drink thanks to its unique taste, clever advertising, and a passion for innovation.

Our Global Reach

Coca-Cola operates in over 200 countries, serving 1.9 billion drinks daily. With more than 500 brands, it tailors products to local tastes, from Fanta Exotic in Africa to Ayataka Tea in Japan. Partnering with 250 bottlers, it produces locally to meet regional needs. Through its World Without Waste initiative, Coca-Cola aims to recycle every bottle it sells by 2030, reflecting its commitment to sustainability. Globally, it contributes to local economies, providing jobs and supporting community development through education, health, and empowerment initiatives.

Coca-Cola and Popular Culture

Coca-Cola is more than a beverage; it has become a symbol of happiness, joy, and celebration. The iconic logo and bottle shape are instantly recognizable worldwide. Coca-Cola has been a pop culture icon for over a century, featuring in movies, music, and art. The brand's iconic Santa Claus ads helped shape the modern image of Santa, and its "I'd Like to Buy the World a Coke" campaign became a cultural milestone. Coca-Cola’s recognizable logo and bottle design are symbols of global pop culture, making appearances in everything from Andy Warhol's art to major films.

Timeline of Coca-Cola

  • 1886: Coca-Cola is invented by John Stith Pemberton.
  • 1892: The Coca-Cola Company is founded.
  • 1915: The contour bottle is introduced, becoming an iconic symbol.
  • 1985: Coca-Cola introduces "New Coke," which is later reverted back.
  • 2009: Coca-Cola celebrates its 123rd anniversary.
